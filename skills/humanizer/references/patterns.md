# 24 AI Writing Patterns — Full Reference

Source: Wikipedia:Signs of AI writing (WikiProject AI Cleanup)

---

## CONTENT PATTERNS

### 1. Significance Inflation
**Tells:** "stands as", "serves as a testament", "vital/pivotal/crucial role", "underscores its importance", "reflects broader", "setting the stage for", "indelible mark"

**Problem:** AI inflates the importance of ordinary facts by attaching meaning statements.

❌ "The training session marked a pivotal moment in his fitness journey, underscoring the community's commitment to excellence."
✅ "He showed up. That was the hard part."

---

### 2. Notability Overemphasis
**Tells:** "covered by national media", "active social media presence", "written by a leading expert", "cited in major publications"

**Problem:** AI claims importance instead of demonstrating it.

❌ "Her approach has been covered by regional media outlets and has an active social media presence."
✅ "She posted the workout on Thursday. By Sunday, 4,000 people had shared it."

---

### 3. Superficial -ing Phrases
**Tells:** "highlighting/underscoring/emphasizing X", "ensuring Y", "reflecting Z", "contributing to W", "showcasing V"

**Problem:** Tacked-on -ing phrases that add words without meaning.

❌ "He completed his 100-day challenge, showcasing the power of consistency."
✅ "He completed his 100-day challenge."

---

### 4. Promotional Language
**Tells:** "boasts", "vibrant", "rich [figurative]", "breathtaking", "nestled", "in the heart of", "groundbreaking", "renowned", "must-visit", "stunning"

**Problem:** AI defaults to ad-copy adjectives.

❌ "Nestled in the heart of the vibrant fitness community, Kaizen offers a groundbreaking approach."
✅ "Kaizen is a 100-day program built around daily habits."

---

### 5. Vague Attributions (Weasel Words)
**Tells:** "Experts argue", "Industry reports suggest", "Observers have cited", "Some critics believe"

**Problem:** AI attributes opinions to unnamed authorities.

❌ "Experts believe the Kaizen method has a lasting impact on habits."
✅ "A 2024 meta-analysis in Behaviour Research found that 90-day habit commitments outperform shorter programs."

---

### 6. "Challenges and Future Prospects" Section
**Tells:** "Despite its success, X faces challenges", "Despite these challenges", "Future Outlook", "Moving forward"

**Problem:** Formulaic section AI adds to appear balanced.

❌ "Despite its success, the program faces challenges typical of online fitness communities. Despite these challenges, Kaizen continues to thrive."
✅ [just describe what actually happened or what's actually hard]

---

## LANGUAGE & GRAMMAR PATTERNS

### 7. AI Vocabulary Overload
**High-frequency tells:** Additionally, align with, crucial, delve, emphasizing, enduring, enhance, fostering, garner, highlight (verb), interplay, intricate/intricacies, key (adj), landscape (abstract), pivotal, showcase, tapestry, testament, underscore, valuable, vibrant

❌ "Additionally, it's crucial that we delve into the intricate tapestry of habit formation."
✅ "Habit formation is complicated. Here's how it actually works."

---

### 8. Copula Avoidance ("serves as" instead of "is")
**Tells:** "serves as", "stands as", "marks", "represents", "boasts", "features", "offers"

**Problem:** AI replaces simple "is/are" with elaborate substitutions.

❌ "The gym serves as a community hub and boasts over 200 members."
✅ "The gym is a community hub with 200 members."

---

### 9. Negative Parallelisms
**Tells:** "Not only X but Y", "It's not just about X, it's Y", "Not merely A but B"

❌ "It's not just about lifting weights. It's about building a better version of yourself."
✅ "Lifting weights is the method. The goal is a life you don't need to escape from."

---

### 10. Rule of Three Overuse
**Tells:** Three adjectives, three nouns, or three ideas forced together.

❌ "The program offers innovation, inspiration, and industry-leading insights."
✅ "The program works because it's boring — you do the same thing every day."

---

### 11. Elegant Variation (Synonym Cycling)
**Problem:** AI rotates synonyms to avoid repetition, creating weird shifts.

❌ "The protagonist trains hard. The main character struggles. The central figure perseveres."
✅ "He trains hard. He struggles. He keeps going."

---

### 12. False Ranges ("from X to Y")
**Problem:** X and Y aren't actually on a meaningful scale.

❌ "From the first rep to the final transformation, from struggle to strength."
✅ "The program starts with basic habits and ends with a fundamentally different relationship with your body."

---

## STYLE PATTERNS

### 13. Em Dash Overuse
**Tells:** Em dashes (—) used more than humans would.

❌ "The results—surprising as they were—showed—as we expected—that consistency wins."
✅ "The results showed what we expected: consistency wins."

---

### 14. Bullet Point Overuse
**Problem:** AI converts naturally flowing ideas into lists that don't need to be lists.

❌
- The program is effective.
- It builds habits.
- It creates community.

✅ "The program works because it builds habits in a community that holds you accountable."

---

### 15. Compound Adjective Proliferation
**Tells:** "cutting-edge", "best-in-class", "industry-leading", "world-class", "state-of-the-art"

❌ "This cutting-edge, best-in-class, industry-leading program..."
✅ "This program..."

---

### 16. Throat-Clearing Intros
**Tells:** "In today's fast-paced world...", "In an era where...", "It goes without saying...", "As we all know..."

❌ "In today's fast-paced world, finding time to exercise can be challenging."
✅ "Most people struggle to exercise consistently. Here's what actually helps."

---

## AI DISCOURSE PATTERNS

### 17. Excessive Transitional Meta-Commentary
**Tells:** "It is important to note that", "It is worth noting", "It should be mentioned that"

❌ "It is important to note that consistency is key."
✅ "Consistency is key."

---

### 18. Lists Without Context
**Problem:** AI creates bullet lists where narrative would be clearer.

❌
Benefits of the Kaizen program:
- Builds habits
- Creates accountability
- Improves fitness

✅ "Kaizen builds habits first, which creates accountability naturally, which eventually improves fitness."

---

### 19. Diplomatic Softening
**Tells:** "It could be argued", "some might say", "one could suggest"

❌ "It could be argued that morning workouts are more effective."
✅ "Morning workouts are more effective for most people. Here's why."

---

### 20. Incomplete Information Disclaimers
**Tells:** "While specific details are not extensively documented...", "While information is limited..."

❌ "While specific details about the program's founding are not extensively documented, it appears to have started in 2018."
✅ "The program started in 2018." (or say you don't know)

---

### 21. Sycophantic/Servile Tone
**Tells:** "Great question!", "You're absolutely right!", "Excellent point!"

❌ "Great question! You're absolutely right that this is a complex topic."
✅ [Just answer the question]

---

## FILLER & HEDGING

### 22. Filler Phrases
| Before | After |
|--------|-------|
| "In order to achieve this goal" | "To achieve this" |
| "Due to the fact that" | "Because" |
| "At this point in time" | "Now" |
| "In the event that" | "If" |
| "The system has the ability to" | "The system can" |
| "It is important to note that" | [delete it] |
| "With that being said" | [delete it] |
| "Moving forward" | [delete it] |

---

### 23. Excessive Hedging
❌ "It could potentially possibly be argued that the program might have some effect on outcomes."
✅ "The program improves outcomes for most participants."

---

### 24. Generic Positive Conclusions
**Tells:** "The future looks bright", "exciting times lie ahead", "a major step in the right direction"

❌ "The future looks bright for the Lazy Lifter community as they continue their journey toward excellence."
✅ "Season 10 opens in July. Waitlist is at join.cholim.ph."
