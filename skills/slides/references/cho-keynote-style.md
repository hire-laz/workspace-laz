# Cho's Keynote Style Guide

**Source:** Lesson 7 (test) + Slide Style PDFs from Cho (2026-05-12)
**Purpose:** Replicate Cho's EXACT presentation style for all future keynote/lesson slides

---

## Color Palette

| Role | Value | Usage |
|------|-------|-------|
| **Background** | `#0A0A0A` / `#111111` (near-black) | All slides — solid dark bg |
| **Primary Text** | `#FFFFFF` (white) | Headings, body, all main text |
| **Secondary Text** | `#D0D0D0` (light gray) | Supporting text, captions |
| **Accent** | `#B6FF00` / `#A8FF00` (neon lime green) | Key phrases, emphasis, highlights |
| **NO other colors** | — | Monochrome + one accent only |

## Typography

| Level | Style | Size | Weight |
|-------|-------|------|--------|
| **Title / Hook** | ALL CAPS, condensed sans-serif | 36-48pt | ExtraBold / Black |
| **Section Header** | ALL CAPS or Title Case | 28-36pt | Bold |
| **Body / Bullets** | Sentence case, clean sans | 18-24pt | Regular / Semibold |
| **Branding** | "Kaizen - More Than Just Fitness™" | 12-14pt | Regular |

**Font direction:** Condensed bold sans-serif (Bebas Neue, Anton, Oswald, Montserrat ExtraBold, or Inter Black)

## Layout Archetypes

### 1. Title / Section Divider
- Dark bg, centered HUGE text
- 1-3 lines max
- Tons of whitespace
- Branding top/bottom

### 2. Content / Teaching Slide
- Left-aligned bullets OR centered statements
- Very few bullet points (3-5 max)
- Short sentences, one idea per bullet
- Green accent on key terms

### 3. Comparison / VS Slide
- Two-column split
- "VS" in center
- Left = easy/default, Right = hard/growth
- Minimal labels, possibly icons

### 4. Framework / Roadmap Slide
- Timeline or progression diagram
- Milestones: Week 1 → Week 2 → Week 3 → Day 100
- Labels: Foundation, Program, Adjustments, Next Level
- "You v1.0 → You v2.0" transformation arc

### 5. Question / Engagement Slide
- Single rhetorical question, large
- Centered, dramatic spacing
- Sometimes repeated for emphasis

### 6. Definition / Science Slide
- One key term defined
- Clean, minimal layout
- Green highlight on the term

## Image Treatment
- High-contrast photos on dark backgrounds
- Desaturated or darkened
- Full-bleed for section openers
- Cutout portraits/fitness imagery
- Text overlay: bold white uppercase, dark overlay beneath if needed
- NO stock photo feel — authentic, raw, powerful

## Lesson Structure (Sequencing)
1. **Hook** — Big promise / dramatic title (MOTIVATION HACK, GODLIKE FOCUS)
2. **Agenda** — What we'll cover (bullet list)
3. **Engagement** — Rhetorical questions to prime audience
4. **Framework** — Transformation roadmap (Day 100 progression)
5. **Science** — Biological/psychological explanation (dopamine, neuroscience)
6. **Proof** — Vivid example/experiment (rat study, real cases)
7. **Application** — How to apply this to your life
8. **Comparison** — Easy vs Hard activities (VS slide)
9. **Summary** — Key takeaways, identity upgrade language

## Recurring Motifs
- **Branding repetition** — "Kaizen - More Than Just Fitness™" on every slide
- **ALL CAPS emphasis** — HIGH MOTIVATION, GODLIKE FOCUS, LESS IS MORE
- **Science + self-help fusion** — Neuroscience terms + motivational language
- **Identity transformation** — "You version 1.0 → 2.0"
- **Dramatic pacing** — One idea per slide, suspense building

## Tone
- Bold, punchy, high-energy
- Direct and conversational (Tagalog + English code-switching)
- Scientific credibility + motivational urgency
- Performance-oriented, masculine energy
- Clean, no clutter, no decoration for decoration's sake

## Anti-Patterns (NEVER DO for Cho's style)
- ❌ Light/white backgrounds
- ❌ Pastel or soft colors
- ❌ Multiple accent colors
- ❌ Dense text paragraphs
- ❌ Decorative icons or emoji
- ❌ Rounded cute elements
- ❌ Gradient backgrounds
- ❌ Stock photo aesthetic
